Dear Athletica.

June 2026

I'm applying for the AI Full-Stack Developer role. I'll keep this short — because the work speaks louder.

I've spent the last two years building PadelEye — an AI-native platform that manages tournaments, tracks player progression, and delivers performance analytics for padel. It's live, it's been used in 6 real tournaments and 22+ individual sessions, and it's currently mid-migration from a GAS backend to a proper SQL + API stack.

Before PadelEye, I spent 6+ years as Product Owner of Cão Carioca — a PHP SaaS with 50+ controllers, PIX and Asaas payment integrations, and thousands of live records hosted on Vultr. The new ERP version is being rebuilt in Laravel, which is where most of my current PHP focus sits.

My background isn't a traditional dev path. I came from 20 years in business — 10 in corporate supply chain at Seadrill, then a decade building products at TechNigma AI Lab — and crossed into hands-on development through necessity and passion. Sports have been constant throughout: soccer, padel, and now building tools for the world I grew up competing in.

On AI workflow: Claude Code, Cursor, Copilot, and Gemini are daily tools — not experiments. As a matter of fact, this presentation was built using Claude, MS Copilot, GitHub, Vercel, and HTML — tools that can only be put together at this pace if you have deep understanding of how AI works. I did revise it all with my human eyes, each step of the way. I like to get the best out of each resource, either the human or the computer. This is me.

Every module in PadelEye was built AI-native from day one. That's the only way I work, and it's the reason I can ship faster, iterate tighter, and maintain higher quality than a traditional full stack dev. And if we do this as a team, then we can go as far as we can imagine, at the speed of quant.

Athletica's mission — adaptable coaching for real athletes — is exactly the product space I'm already in. I'd love to bring that energy to your team.

At this point in my career path, networking with the USA and Europe, and hands-on experience in AI development counts more to me than a big paycheck. I understand the capital constraints a startup undergoes at each stage of growth, and I'm willing to fit into the available budget.
